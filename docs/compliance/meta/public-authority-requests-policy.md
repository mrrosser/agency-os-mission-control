# Public Authority Data Request Policy

- Owner: Rosser NFT Gallery
- Applies to: Platform Data and personal data received from Meta APIs
- Effective date: 2026-02-12
- Review cadence: Annual or on material legal/process change

## 1. Purpose

This policy defines how Rosser NFT Gallery handles requests from public authorities for Platform Data and related personal data obtained via Meta integrations.

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to:

- Requests from government agencies, regulators, or national security authorities.
- Data handled in systems operated by Rosser NFT Gallery and its service providers.
- Meta-related data categories, including identifiers, tokens, profile/account metadata, and related operational records.

## 3. Roles and responsibilities

- Data Controller: Rosser NFT Gallery.
- Compliance reviewer: Designated by Rosser NFT Gallery leadership.
- Technical responder: Authorized operator with least-privilege access.
- Final approver: Owner or delegated legal/compliance authority.

## 4. Legality review requirement

No disclosure is made unless a legality review is completed and documented.

Minimum review checks:

1. Requesting authority identity and authenticity.
2. Legal basis, jurisdiction, and scope.
3. Specificity of requested accounts/data and time range.
4. Whether disclosure is required by applicable law.

If required information is missing or unclear, Rosser NFT Gallery requests clarification before any response.

## 5. Challenging unlawful or overbroad requests

Rosser NFT Gallery will challenge or narrow requests when legally permitted and when requests appear:

- Unlawful under applicable law.
- Overbroad relative to stated legal basis.
- Insufficiently specific.

Challenge actions may include:

1. Requesting narrower scope.
2. Requesting judicial/legal documentation.
3. Deferring disclosure pending clarification.

All challenge actions are recorded in the request log.

## 6. Data minimization and least disclosure

Rosser NFT Gallery discloses only the minimum information necessary to satisfy a valid request.

Controls:

1. Filter by account, data type, and date range.
2. Exclude unrelated tenant and user data.
3. Prefer redaction where full values are not required.
4. Do not disclose secrets or credentials unless legally required and approved.

## 7. Documentation and audit trail

Each request must be logged in `public-authority-request-log.csv` (or equivalent secure register) with:

- Request ID and date received.
- Requesting authority and jurisdiction.
- Legal basis cited.
- Data requested and data disclosed.
- Legal reasoning summary.
- Decision and approver.
- Date completed and retention date.

Supporting artifacts (notice, legal docs, correspondence, disclosure package) are retained with controlled access.

## 8. Service providers with access to Platform Data

Current processors/service providers:

- Google LLC (Google Cloud Platform).
- Clerk, Inc.
- OpenAI, L.L.C.

Processor access follows contractual and operational controls and is limited to service delivery.

## 9. Security and access controls

1. Least-privilege access to systems containing requested data.
2. Encrypted storage and encrypted transfer when responding.
3. Logged administrative access and action traceability.

## 10. Retention

Request records and response artifacts are retained according to Rosser NFT Gallery data retention practices and legal obligations.

## 11. Exceptions

Exceptions require written approval by the Final Approver and must be logged with rationale.
